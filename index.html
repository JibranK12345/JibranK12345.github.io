  • Peeko

    Background :
    As a highschool student, I was noticing social networking was becoming more and more inauthetnic. My friends were posting to seek attention, garner likes, and advertise how perfect their lives were. Social media was intended for communication, and it had instead devolved into a depressing bubble filled with falsfied, filtered images. So, I decided to develop Peeko, a platform for authetnicty through anonymous conversations with your friends about the topics that are really on your mind.

    Overview:
    Peeko allows users to add friends and post topics anonymously about whatever's on their mind; all their friends can see the topic, but no one will know it's them. If the topic gets enough "peeks", the user's identity will be revelaed. Otherwise, the topic will disappear after 6, 12, or 24 hours. Friends can start conversations with the topic, anonymously; if the topic is revealed, both users in the conversation will have their identity revealed. The algorithm to determine how many peeks a post needs to get revealed is based off a percentage of total friends and total active friends.

    Technology:
    Peeko was built using Swift, Firebase, Google Cloud Storage, and node.JS Google Cloud Functions. Google Cloud Functions are used in multiple ways, such as to delete old posts (this function is run every minute), append information to a revealed post, report users, etc. Caching is used to store friend lists, messages, and images. Google Cloud Storage is used to store profile images and user posted images. Peeko includes custom UI, logic to ensure syncing between cached data and new data, and a signup flow. Peeko is my largest project to date, and development took 1.5 years.



  • Scan To Sheets

    Background / Overview:
    In Mu Alpha Theta, a club I was a part of in high school, attendance would take at the end of meetings would take over ten minutes. So, I made Scan To Sheets; it allowed officers to simply scan a student's ID and have their information instantly uploaded to a Google Sheet. Scan To Sheets began expanding rapidly; at first, other clubs in my highschool began using it. Soona after, schools within the district, and, within a few months, people — specifically teachers — in over 60 countries had started using the app for attendance. My math teacher asked if I could release an update that would allow users to scan two barcodes and have them both uplaoded, as our school was begining to check out Apple Pencils to students. I released the update, and even more teachers began using the app for checking out Apple Pencils. What started as an app for a school club had expanded globally.

    Technology:
    Scan To Sheets was developed in Swift and uses the Google Sheets API to upload barcodes and QR codes instantaneously to a Google Sheet.
